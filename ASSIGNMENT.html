Horticultural and Medicinal Plants of Northeast India

Introduction

Northeast India is one of the richest biodiversity hotspots in the world. The region supports a wide variety of horticultural and medicinal plants due to its favorable climate, fertile soils, and abundant rainfall.

Major Horticultural Plants

Pineapple

Ananas comosus

Important commercial fruit crop of Northeast India.

Orange

Citrus reticulata

Popular citrus fruit grown extensively in Meghalaya.

Banana

Musa spp.

Widely cultivated throughout the region.

Kiwi

Actinidia deliciosa

Commercially grown in Arunachal Pradesh.

Ginger

Zingiber officinale

Major spice crop with high export potential.

Turmeric

Curcuma longa

Known for medicinal and culinary uses.

Tea

Camellia sinensis

Assam is world famous for tea production.

Orchid

Orchidaceae family

Northeast India is home to numerous orchid species.

Major Medicinal Plants

Tulsi

Ocimum sanctum

Used for treating cold, cough and fever.

Aloe Vera

Aloe barbadensis

Used in skin care and wound healing.

Ashwagandha

Withania somnifera

Known for stress relief and immunity enhancement.

Brahmi

Bacopa monnieri

Improves memory and cognitive functions.

Sarpagandha

Rauwolfia serpentina

Used for management of hypertension.

Neem

Azadirachta indica

Possesses antibacterial and antifungal properties.
